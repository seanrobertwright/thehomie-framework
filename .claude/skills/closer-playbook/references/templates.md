# Message Bank — adapt, never send verbatim

Each template needs: the client's name, their stated reason (if any), the
live links (preview + offer page), and a REAL deadline. Personalize with
their words; keep ONE decision-shaped ask.

## Courtesy note (ladder rung 1 — missed check-in)

> Good morning [Name]. [Yesterday/Friday] was our check-in date so this is
> just a courtesy note. Your preview alone already puts you ahead of the
> game: [2-3 concrete things built, e.g. "new site, your AI concierge, and
> your lead dashboard"]. [preview link]
>
> We take driven entrepreneurs and put them toe to toe with the enterprise
> players. That level of work is why I only take a few builds a month. If
> you're in, it's half now, half at delivery: [offer link]
>
> [Their-reason line, e.g. "Hope the credit line came through."] If things
> are still tight, be straight with me, my finance team can help you set up
> a payment plan that fits your budget. Let me know by [real deadline].

## Cold close-out (ladder rung 2 — silence past deadline)

> Hey [Name], since I didn't hear back, I'm going to close this out on my
> end. I only take on a few projects at a time, and I need clients who are
> responsive and ready to move. Wishing you the best.

Nothing follows this. If they come back, they come back to OUR calendar.

## Deposit nudge (said yes, hasn't paid)

> [Name], great talking today. Everything's set on my end. Your start is
> half now, half at delivery, and the button's on your page: [offer link].
> The moment the deposit lands you're on this week's build schedule. Which
> plan are we locking in?

## Delivery-balance ask (build delivered, balance due)

> [Name], your build is delivered and live: [link]. That's the full
> [tier name] as promised. The delivery balance is the last step:
> [balance link]. Once that's in I flip everything to your ownership and we
> schedule your go-live walkthrough. Today work?

## Finance-plan reply (price stall)

> Totally hear you, [Name]. That's exactly why I have a finance team. Be
> straight with me on what monthly works and they'll set up a plan that fits
> your budget. The build quality doesn't change, just the payment shape.
> Want me to have them draw it up?

## Kickoff confirm (deposit received)

> [Name], deposit received. You're officially on the build schedule. Here's
> what happens next: [2-3 concrete steps + dates]. I'll need [one thing from
> them] by [date] to stay on pace. Welcome aboard, excited to build this one
> with you.

## Forwardable summary (spouse/partner objection)

> Here's the one-paragraph version for [partner name]: YourProduct builds
> [Name]'s [website/AI receptionist/growth system] — the same machine the
> big [industry] brands run, sized for [business name]. [Tier] is [$X] to
> start (half now, half at delivery) and [$Y]/month after go-live, month to
> month. The preview is already live at [link]. I only take a few builds a
> month, and [Name]'s spot is this month's schedule.
